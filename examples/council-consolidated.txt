This is a genuinely common parenting decision, and there's no single right answer — it depends on your child and your family. Here are the key things to think through:

**Consider your child's readiness**
- Do they demonstrate responsibility with other things (chores, schoolwork, belongings)?
- Can they handle disappointment and limits without escalating?
- Do you have a reasonable level of trust in their judgment?

**Practical reasons a phone might make sense at 10**
- Safety and the ability to reach you (especially if they're home alone or traveling independently)
- Coordinating activities and playdates
- Social inclusion, depending on their peer group

**Real concerns worth taking seriously**
- Screen time and its impact on sleep, focus, and mood
- Exposure to social media, inappropriate content, and online strangers
- The social pressures that come with constant connectivity

**A middle-ground approach many families find useful**
- Start with a basic/limited phone (calls and texts only) rather than a full smartphone
- Use parental controls and screen time limits from day one
- Set clear rules upfront: where the phone goes at night, what apps are allowed, and what happens if rules are broken
- Frame it as a privilege that expands with demonstrated responsibility

**The honest bottom line:** Ten is on the younger end, and many child development experts suggest waiting until 12–14 for a full smartphone. But a simple device for safety and communication is a reasonable choice for many 10-year-olds with the right guardrails in place.

Trust your knowledge of your own kid — you're in the best position to judge their maturity.
